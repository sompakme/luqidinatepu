What Museums Are Open On Tuesday In NYC

People are currently reading this guide.

So You Want to Museum-ize Your Tuesday in NYC? Don't Worry, We've Got You Covered (Unless You Want to See Dinosaur Bones)

Ah, Tuesday. The day that simultaneously feels like a delightful post-Monday sigh of relief and a rude awakening to the fact that the weekend is still a distant mirage. But fear not, fellow New Yorker (or intrepid tourist)! For even on this transitional Tuesday, there's a whole world of culture and (hopefully) air conditioning waiting to be explored within the hallowed halls of NYC's museums.

But Wait... Not All Museums Are Created Equal (Especially on Tuesdays)

Yes, friends, the museum landscape can be a tricky beast on a Tuesday. Some bastions of art and history fling their doors wide open, while others remain stubbornly shut, their treasures hidden away like a toddler's prized collection of boogers. But fret not, for we've compiled a battle plan to conquer your Tuesday museum cravings!

The Classics: Open and Ready to Wow You (Maybe)

  • The Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met): Get your fill of everything from Egyptian sarcophagi to Van Gogh's sunflowers (just don't try to eat them). Be warned, this place is massive, so plan your attack or you might get lost amidst the mummies.
  • The Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum: This architectural marvel, swirling upwards like a giant ice cream cone, is home to an impressive collection of modern and contemporary art. Perfect for pondering the meaning of life while avoiding getting dizzy.
  • The Morgan Library & Museum: Step into a bygone era of opulent book collections and historical artifacts. Just remember, whispering is highly encouraged, unless of course, you unearth a hidden map to buried pirate treasure (then feel free to scream with glee).

Beyond the Big Names: Hidden Gems for the Discerning Tuesday Explorer

  • The Tenement Museum: Take a trip back in time and explore the lives of 19th and early 20th-century immigrants in a historic tenement building. Just try not to feel too smug about your fancy modern plumbing.
  • The Museum of the City of New York: Delve into the rich tapestry of New York City's history, from its humble beginnings to its current incarnation as the city that never sleeps (or maybe just never stops honking its horn).
  • The National Museum of the American Indian: Explore the diverse cultures and histories of Native American peoples. Tuesdays are actually free for Native Americans, so be respectful and mindful of others.

Important Side Note: Dinosaur Fans, Look Away

Unfortunately, our beloved Museum of Natural History is closed on Tuesdays. So, if your heart is set on gawking at giant prehistoric bones, you'll have to adjust your plans. Maybe consider a Netflix documentary about T-Rex on your Tuesday instead?

There You Have It, Folks!

Now you're armed with the knowledge to conquer your Tuesday museum blues. Remember, this is just a taste of what NYC's museums have to offer. So, lace up your walking shoes, grab your favorite museum buddy (or podcast, no judgement here), and get ready to be cultured (or at least marvel at some really expensive-looking paintings).

Happy museum-ing!
