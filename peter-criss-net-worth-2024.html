Peter Criss’ Early Life

Criss was born in Brooklyn, New York, and developed a passion for music at a young age. He began playing the drums in his early teens and played in various bands before co-founding Kiss in the early 1970s.

Music Career with Kiss

Criss was a key member of Kiss, contributing not only his drumming skills but also his songwriting abilities. He co-wrote and sang lead vocals on some of the band’s biggest hits, including “Beth” and “Black Diamond”. His unique style and persona as the “Catman” were integral to the band’s image and success.

Solo Career

After leaving Kiss in the early 1980s, Criss embarked on a solo career. He released several albums and singles, some of which achieved moderate success. His solo work further contributed to his wealth and solidified his status as a respected musician in the rock genre.

Reunion with Kiss

In the late 1990s, Criss reunited with Kiss for a series of successful tours and album releases. These ventures were highly profitable and significantly boosted his net worth.

Retirement and Later Ventures

Criss officially retired from live performances in 2017, but he continues to earn income from his music through royalties and licensing deals. He has also ventured into other areas, such as authoring a memoir and participating in various television and film projects.

Real Estate Investments

Over the years, Criss has made several savvy real estate investments. He has owned properties in upscale areas such as Beverly Hills and Greenwich, Connecticut, which have likely appreciated in value over time.

Merchandising and Endorsements

As a member of Kiss, Criss has benefited from the band’s extensive merchandising efforts. From action figures to comic books, the Kiss brand is a merchandising powerhouse. Additionally, Criss has had endorsement deals with various companies, particularly those related to music and drumming equipment.

Charitable Work

Criss is also known for his charitable work, particularly in support of breast cancer awareness. While this does not directly contribute to his net worth, it is an important aspect of his public image and legacy.
