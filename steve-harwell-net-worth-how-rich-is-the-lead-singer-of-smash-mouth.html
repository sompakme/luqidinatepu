Steve Harwell net worth: How rich is the lead singer of Smash Mouth?

Former Smash Mouth lead signer Steve Harwell has entered hospice care. This development comes as a result of an extended struggle with various health problems, that led to Harwell's retirement from music in 2021.

On Sunday, the band's manager, Robert Hayes, shared the sad news that Harwell is now in a hospice, being looked after by his fiancee, as he faces the final stages of chronic liver failure, with just around a week left to live.

"Steve's iconic voice is one of the most recognizable voices from his generation," Haye said.

"Steve loved the fans and loved to perform. Although Steve is here with us still, sadly it will only be for a short time.

"My only additional comment is that we would hope that people would respect Steve and his family's privacy during this difficult time."

What are Steve Harwell's health problems?

In 2013, Harwell was diagnosed with cardiomyopathy, a cardiac ailment characterized by the enlargement and weakening of the heart muscle, as well as a severe neurological condition known as Wernicke encephalopathy.

Later, in August 2016, during a Smash Mouth performance in Urbana, Illinois, he suffered a dramatic onstage collapse, leading to his immediate transportation to the hospital via an ambulance.

One year later, Smash Mouth found it necessary to postpone several of their concerts due to his ongoing treatment for his heart-related health conditions.

What is Smash Mouth singer Steve Harwell's net worth?

Harwell has accumulated a net worth of 2.5 million dollars, thanks to his successful music career.

He is most recognized for his role as a co-founder and the lead vocalist of the renowned band Smash Mouth.

He, alongside bassist Paul De Lisle, has been a part of the band since its inception, making them the sole surviving original members.

Back in 1999, Steve Harwell reportedly invested 600,000 dollars in a residence located in Henderson, Nevada.

He later decided to sell this property in 2009, fetching 800,000 dollars from the sale. Following this transaction, he relocated to Nashville, Tennessee.
