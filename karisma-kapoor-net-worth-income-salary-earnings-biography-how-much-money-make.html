Karisma Kapoor (born June 25, 1974), often known as Lolo, is an Indian actress who has appeared in Bollywood films. She is the daughter of actors Randhir Kapoor and Babita Kapoor and is a member of the Kapoor family. In 1991, at the age of seventeen, Kapoor made her acting debut in Prem Qaidi. Jigar (1992), Anari (1993), Raja Babu, Suhaag (1994), Coolie No. 1, Gopi Kishan (1995), Saajan Chale Sasural & Jeet (1995) were among her financially successful flicks (1996). Raja Hindustani, her greatest financial hit, awarded Kapoor her first Filmfare Award for Best Actress in 1996, and she subsequently got the National Film Award for Best Supporting Actress for her portrayal in the love drama Dil To Pagal Hai (1997). She went on to star in the highly acclaimed films Fiza (2000) and  Zubeidaa (2001), for which she won the Filmfare awards for Best Actress and Best Actress (Critics) respectively. Kapoor has established herself as one of Hindi cinema’s premier actresses as a result of her achievements. She is regarded as one of Bollywood’s most gorgeous ladies and a sex symbol. Kapoor has garnered one National Film Award and four Filmfare Awards over her career, in addition to six nominations. In addition to performing in films, Kapoor has also appeared as a talent judge on the reality programmes Nach Baliye and Hans Baliye and in the television series Karishma – The Miracles of Destiny (2003). Kapoor has been a national style symbol in the last ten years. Kapoor married Sanjay Kapoor in 2003 after a high-profile romance with actor Abhishek Bachchan.
