Early Life

Peter Strauss was born on February 20, 1947, in Croton-on-Hudson, New York. He attended the Hackley School for Boys in Tarrytown, New York, and later graduated from Northwestern University in 1969. Career Beginnings

Strauss made his acting debut in the 1970s, with minor roles in films and television series. His breakthrough came in 1976 when he starred in the television miniseries “Rich Man, Poor Man.” His performance earned him an Emmy Award and catapulted him to fame.

Peak of His Career

Following the success of “Rich Man, Poor Man,” Strauss became a household name in the United States. He starred in several successful miniseries, including “Masada,” “Kane & Abel,” and “Tender is the Night.” His performances in these series further solidified his status as a leading actor in the television industry.

Later Career and Retirement
